Current State
What's broken, what's holding it together, what the board is watching.
Aging infrastructure — ~25% of storage arrays are 5+ years old, with several past end-of-support across three primary U.S. data centers and the Atlanta Operations Control Center.
Hybrid complexity — Four primary storage platforms across Microsoft Azure (primary cloud), AWS (select workloads), on-prem (ATL/MSP), and legacy edge systems at hub stations.
Siloed operational data — Real-time ops data spread across crew, fleet, customer, and ground-handling systems — OCC dashboards lag 5–15 minutes during disruptions.
Resilience gap exposed by CrowdStrike — July 2024 outage produced a multi-day recovery; internal restore time benchmarked at ~10× best-in-class peers. Class actions and DOT scrutiny ongoing.
Backup &amp; ransomware risk — Backup success rate ~91% against a 99% target. Limited immutable copies for ops-critical workloads. Aviation sector is a rising ransomware target.
Multi-cloud cost creep — Combined cloud + storage OpEx growing faster than IT budget. Egress and storage tiering not optimized across providers; visibility limited.
