About

I keep the monitoring running at Meridian — detection, dashboards, alerting, and the Splunk and Instana stacks everyone quietly depends on until something slips through. I've owned monitoring here through the whole Heritage integration, which means I'm the person who knows why the two core systems log differently and which alerts you can and can't trust. Most of my week is the unglamorous stuff behind every "resilience" headline: tuning noise out of the queue, chasing failed log pipelines, and watching an ingest bill that keeps climbing faster than anyone upstairs would like. Came up as a hands-on operator, not a slide-maker. So when a vendor pitches me, I'm going to test the claim on the spot — give me the false-positive rate, the time-to-detect on a real bad change, what it costs at our volume. Bring me numbers and I'll champion you internally. Bring me adjectives and I'll smile and end the meeting early. When I'm not staring at dashboards I shoot black-and-white film photography — mostly street stuff around Charlotte on weekends. It's the one thing in my life with no alerts. Report into Daniel Hughes. Always glad to compare notes with other observability and monitoring folks.
